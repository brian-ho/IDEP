    Detailed Information

    The following is more detailed information about this study in addition to the information listed above.

    What is the purpose of this research?

    The purpose of this research is to understand how people perceive the physical form of the built environment and cities. We are interested to see if certain kinds of city form change people’s perceptions and experience — i.e. if a part of the city makes you want to walk further, or if it feel like an enclosed neighborhood. We are also interested to see what kinds of city form are more memorable, and get a sense for what characteristics of a city’s visual image are important to human perception.

    How long will I take part in this research?

    Each trial will take about 10 minutes, and you may repeat the survey if you like..

    What can I expect if I take part in this research?

    You will be asked to label sets of street-level images of urban environments; after completing this section you will then be shown a sequence of individual images — some of which have been seen previously in the first section — and indicate whether they recall having seen the image beforehand. Finally, you will have a choice to share some of you own memories of the city of Boston.

    What happens if I say yes, but I change my mind later?

    You can leave the research at any time it will not be held against you. Data collected up to the point of withdrawal will be used for analysis, but if you specific otherwise this data can be removed.

    If I take part in this research, how will my privacy be protected?  What happens to the information you collect?

    Efforts will be made to limit the use and disclosure of your personal information, including research study, to people who have a need to review this information. We cannot promise complete secrecy. Organizations that may inspect and copy your information include the IRB and other representatives of this organization.

    If identifiers are removed from your identifiable private information or identifiable samples that are collected during this research, that information or those samples could be used for future research studies or distributed to another investigator for future research studies without your additional informed consent.

    If you agree to take part in this research study, you may voluntarily enter into a raffle to win a copy of Kevin Lynch’s Image of the City and a $20 Amazon gift certificate. You will be asked after survey completion to submit an email, which will be used to select and notify one raffle winner.

    The person in charge of the research study or the sponsor can remove you from the research study without your approval. Possible reasons for removal include abuse of the survey or an answer pattern that suggests an incomplete or inaccurate test.
